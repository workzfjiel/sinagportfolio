# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients — startup founders, small-business owners, and creative leads — evaluating whether to hire Johnloyd Patulay (SINAG) for premium landing pages, web apps, or motion graphics. They arrive from a shared link or social profile, skim on desktop or phone, and decide within a minute whether the work looks worth paying for. Secondary: recruiters and peers verifying skills via Credly credentials.

## Product Purpose

SINAG is Johnloyd's personal portfolio and primary sales tool. Its job is to *demonstrate* — not describe — his craft: the site itself is the proof of the "premium digital experiences" he sells. Success = a visitor scrolls the whole page, believes the quality claim, and sends an inquiry through the contact form or email.

## Brand Personality

Moody, cinematic, precise. Dark near-black surfaces, one warm cream accent (#DEDBC8 — "sinag" means *ray of light* in Filipino: cream light against black), restrained serif-italic emphasis, motion that feels choreographed rather than decorated. Confident but not loud.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated landing pages: gradient text, glassmorphism cards, purple-blue SaaS gradients.
- Template portfolios (identical card grids, stock "developer" imagery, emoji-laden hero copy).
- Anything that reads "budget freelancer": default fonts, no motion, visible admin controls.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the portfolio piece.** Every interaction must be shippable to a paying client as-is.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Motion and craft prove the service claims; copy stays short.
3. **One light source.** Cream (#DEDBC8) is the only voice color; everything else is a shade of black. Issuer accents in Credentials are the sole exception.
4. **Premium over practical.** Prefer the more choreographed option when both work (owner's explicit preference).
5. **Invisible seams.** Admin tooling stays hidden behind the secret key sequence; visitors only ever see the polished surface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA: 4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text — on the dark palette this means minimum ~55–60% cream opacity for small text.
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support (heavy motion site — mandatory).
- Custom cursor only on fine-pointer devices; never degrade touch.
- Keyboard: visible `:focus-visible` states, semantic headings, labeled form controls.
